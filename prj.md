## 1. Project Overview

**Name:** Ghats Arcade
**Tagline:** "Where the Western Ghats meet the Palakkad Gap. A farmland for your family."

**Brand voice:**
Warm and familial. Poetic and lifestyle-led. Transparent and process-detailed. Theme-driven and culturally rooted. Never corporate. Never pushy. Always honest.

**One-sentence positioning:**
A theme-based, gated community of managed farmlands on the Kerala–Tamil Nadu border — clear in titles, engineered in infrastructure, and managed by people who actually live here.

**Purpose:**
Ghats Arcade is the marketing, lead-generation, and CRM platform for a managed agri-farmland business rooted in Eruthempathy, Palakkad — on the Kerala side of the Palakkad Gap, 80 km from Coimbatore. We source dryland parcels, lay panchayat-spec roads, secure water, restore a shared heritage clubhouse, plant fruit and agri crops, subdivide into 25-cent plots, and sell them as agricultural land to metro and NRI buyers seeking a weekend retreat, a passive land asset, and a connection to something real.

The site does three things:
1. **Attract** — rank on search engines; pull Gulf/Bangalore/Coimbatore/US/UK buyers into the story
2. **Convert** — move visitors into the CRM through inquiry forms, site-visit bookings, and WhatsApp
3. **Trust** — answer every fear a buyer arrives with before it becomes a reason to walk away

This is not a transactional marketplace. No online payments or e-signing. It is a content-led, lead-generation platform with a backend CRM for the Owner and future Admins.

## 3. Core Features (In Scope)

### 3.1 Public-Facing Website — Page Inventory & Psychological Conversion Layer

> **Psychological design principle applied throughout:**
> A visitor arriving at this site is not ready to buy. They are in one of three mental states:
> - **Dreamer** (curious, browsing, no urgency) — needs inspiration and emotional permission
> - **Researcher** (comparing options, cautious, wants proof) — needs trust signals and transparent information
> - **Decider** (has narrowed down, needs a push) — needs urgency, social proof, and a frictionless next step
>
> The home page serves all three simultaneously. The project detail page serves Researchers and Deciders. The contact and site-visit form is the Decider's threshold.
>
> **Contact information should never be asked cold.** The visitor must first receive value (emotion, information, or social proof) before being asked to give their name and phone number. Every lead capture placement below is deliberately sequenced to follow a value-delivery moment.

**Block 1 — Hero (full-bleed video or landscape photography)**

*Psychological role: First impression, emotional anchor, identity signal*

Real footage of Eruthempathy/Palakkad border terrain — green hills, laterite road, fruit trees, morning light. No stock images. The viewer must feel they are looking at a real place, not a brochure.

*Why two CTAs here:* The Decider clicks Schedule. The Dreamer/Researcher clicks Projects. Both are captured into the right funnel. Do not ask for contact details at this stage — the visitor has given nothing yet and trusts nothing yet.

**Block 2 — "It's your land. But it doesn't have to be your full-time job."**

*Psychological role: Reframe the objection ("I don't have time to manage land") before it forms*

Short poetic prose block. Not a list. A feeling. The copy should make the reader pause and feel seen.

Example copy direction:
*"We prepare the soil. We plant the crops. We water, nurture, and maintain.*
*You arrive when it matters — to pluck fruit from your own trees, to watch your children run between rows of green, to host slow lunches under open Kerala skies.*
*You live the farm life. We manage the farm work."*

CTA: "See How It Works" (links to "What is Managed Farmland?" page)

*No lead capture here.* The visitor is still building their mental model. Interrupting with a form destroys the mood.

---

**Block 3 — Buyer-Fear Reframe Grid**

*Psychological role: Preemptive objection handling — the most trust-building block on the page*

6-panel grid. Each panel: the fear a buyer arrives with (left column) vs. Ghats Arcade's answer (right column). Fears are written in the buyer's own language — not developer-speak.

1. Unclear or disputed land titles → Individual freehold titles, verified by us before we acquire
2. No one to manage land in my absence → Resident team on-site; monthly maintenance; structured management
3. Developers who sell and disappear → We live near this land. Our phone number doesn't change after you register.
4. Can I put a structure on it? → Heritage clubhouse is the shared facility. Individual panchayat permission pathway explained honestly.
5. Plot sizes don't fit my budget → 10-cent to 50-cent plots; you choose what suits your family
6. Hidden charges → Transparent pricing: land price + maintenance fee + registration charges. That's it.

*Why this works psychologically:* Naming the fear yourself is more disarming than ignoring it. Buyers feel heard before they've said a word. This block alone can convert a skeptic to an inquirer.

*No lead capture here.* The work of this block is purely to lower the defensive wall.

Acres developed | Plots sold | Co-farmer families | Years in the region

*Psychological role: Aspiration activation — from investment logic to identity*

Movement (the body follows the land's rhythm). Legacy (where your story continues). Balance (seasons slow you down). Presence (on the farm, every moment asks you to simply be here).

*Why this works:* NRI and metro buyers are not fundamentally buying land. They are buying an identity — the person who owns a farm, who gives their children something real, who stepped off the treadmill. This block gives them permission to want that.

*Still no lead capture.* The emotional peak is being built. The ask comes after the peak, not before.

*Step 1 is the CTA.* The entire 4-step journey is framed around "Schedule a Visit" as the natural first move. The buyer is not being asked to commit — they are being invited to see.

### 3.2 Site-Visit Conversion Psychology — Summary Principles

The goal of the website is not to sell land. It is to get the visitor to agree to a site visit. The site visit sells the land. Everything on the site is a stepping stone to that one commitment.

**The 5 psychological gates a visitor must pass before agreeing to a site visit:**

1. **Emotional resonance** — "This land feels like something I want" (Hero, Holistic Living, About narrative)
2. **Trust** — "This company is legitimate" (Fear reframe, Legal checklist, Team, Google Reviews, Disclaimers)
3. **Social proof** — "Others have done this and are happy" (Video testimonials, Counter, Community strip)
4. **Process clarity** — "I know what happens next" (How to Own 4-step, Registration walkthrough)
5. **Permission** — "It's okay to just visit, I'm not committing to buy" (Step 1 of the journey is always "see it first"; site visit is framed as risk-free)

**The ask must always be framed as low-commitment:** "Schedule a visit" not "Buy now." "We'll walk you through it" not "Sign here." "Come see for yourself" not "Invest today."

**Urgency must be real, not manufactured:** "8 plots remaining in Project 1" is legitimate and effective. "Offer expires in 24 hours" is manipulative and destroys trust with the exact buyer profile we want (educated, cautious NRI/professional).

**Mobile-first capture:** Most Gulf and diaspora traffic arrives on mobile. Every form must be 3–4 fields maximum on mobile. WhatsApp is always the lowest-friction capture channel for this audience — a tap to open a conversation is easier than filling a form.

### 3.3 Admin / CRM Backend

**Authentication**: email/password via Better Auth

**Roles**:
- **Owner** (one only, always): full CRUD all entities, add/delete admins, export all data, manage events, manage lead magnet assets
- **Admin**: CRUD listings, projects, blog posts, events, horticulture logs; view/manage leads + follow-up notes; cannot add/delete admins, cannot export

**Project management**: CRUD for Projects; status: `draft` | `published` | `sold_out` | `coming_soon`

**Plot management**: CRUD for Plots; per-plot status: `available` | `reserved` | `sold`; live units-remaining count displayed publicly

**Listings management**: CRUD standalone listings

**Lead / CRM management**:
- Filter by: status, lead type, source, buyer type, date range
- Lead types: `inquiry` | `site_visit_request` | `callback` | `lead_magnet_download`
- Lead pipeline: New → Contacted → Site Visit Scheduled → Negotiating → Converted / Lost
- **Colour-coded status badges** (`LeadStatusBadge`) in the lead manager and dashboard
- **Dashboard summary cards**: Published Listings / New Leads / Leads in Negotiation / Blog Posts Live
- **Phone deduplication on capture**: repeat submissions from the same normalised phone merge into the existing record (enrich email/WhatsApp, append tagged message history, upgrade `leadType` when a higher-intent capture arrives; phones stored canonically as `+{digits}`)
- Follow-up notes with contact method: WhatsApp / call / email / in-person / site-visit
- One-click WhatsApp from lead record (pre-filled with lead name + project) — **Phase 2** (not yet built; public `wa.me` links are shipped)
- Source attribution: which project / listing / page / blog / form generated the lead
- **"Co-farmer" designation**: once a lead converts, flag as Co-farmer in CRM; enables future community communications
- **Inquiry confirmation email** (optional): when `RESEND_API_KEY` is set, `POST /api/leads` sends a single acknowledgement via Resend; fails soft when unconfigured

**Events management**: CRUD for Events — title, date, project association, description, photos, status (`upcoming` | `past`)

**Horticulture Log**: CRUD per-project and per-plot activity logs

**Gallery / Media management**: upload photos tagged by type; set cover image per project. All media upload is **UI-only** — a non-technical Owner/Admin uploads via drag-and-drop/file-picker in the admin (`MediaUploader`/`GalleryUploader`), never touching the filesystem or Git. Uploads are optimised server-side (`sharp` → WebP for images; PDFs/videos validated against an allowlist + size caps) and filed into named **category folders** under `UPLOAD_DIR` (`projects/`, `listings/`, `blog/`, `events/`, `testimonials/`, `lead-magnets/`, `misc/`) so the media library stays browsable and easy to back up. Storage backend is swappable (local disk / Docker volume now → object storage later) by changing only `src/lib/uploads.ts`. ## 6. Development Methodology

### Current mode: Vibe Coding (AI-assisted rapid iteration)

Development is currently in **vibe-coding mode** — AI-assisted, move-fast, ship-first. The priority is working software and visible progress, not test coverage.

**In this mode:**
- Write code directly. No test-first discipline required.
- Iterate on UI and UX through live feedback, not spec-driven cycles.
- Security-critical paths (auth, role checks) should still be manually verified before any real data is stored, but formal test suites are not required.
- Aim for a working demo as fast as possible.

### Optional: Switch to TDD when handing off to professional developers

When the project moves from solo vibe-coding to a formal development team, enable TDD mode. The full TDD methodology is documented below and can be activated by replacing this section with it.

**TDD summary (activate when needed):**
- Strict Red → Green → Refactor cycle
- No production code without a failing test first
- prj.md is the source of truth for tests
- Security-critical logic (roles, single-Owner invariant, fail-closed default) requires 100% branch coverage in pure extractable modules (e.g., `lib/roles.ts`)
- Test layers: Unit (Vitest) → Component (React Testing Library) → Integration (API + DB) → Regression (on demand) → E2E (Playwright, Phase 2)
